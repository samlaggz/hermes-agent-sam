# Luxury Real-Estate Stock Video Keywords

## Overview

Use this skill to source footage for premium branded real-estate teaser videos. The goal is to avoid generic filler and instead build a coherent luxury sequence from location-correct skyline shots, premium hospitality/lobby moments, interior material details, artisan/craftsmanship close-ups, amenity visuals, and brand-safe lifestyle b-roll.

For Dubai residential projects, footage must feel connected to the project’s actual address, skyline, and amenities. Reject stock that looks like villas, beach resorts, countryside homes, unrelated travel vlogs, watermarked previews, floor plans, or generic lifestyle footage that does not support the property positioning.

## When to Use

- The user asks for a real-estate project teaser or luxury property video.
- The project has a brochure, brand deck, or website and the video must reflect amenities/location.
- The user wants free/no-watermark stock footage from Pexels, Pixabay, Mixkit, Coverr, Videvo, or similar sources.
- The video needs luxury b-roll keyword searches rather than literal building-only footage.
- The user wants elegant, premium typography and short best-segment cuts instead of using full downloaded clips.

Use these when the project is in/near Dubai urban districts, Sheikh Zayed Road, Jumeirah Garden City, Downtown, DIFC, Business Bay, or skyline-facing residences:

- `Dubai skyline cinematic drone`
- `Dubai Sheikh Zayed Road cinematic`
- `Dubai downtown skyline drone sunset`
- `Burj Khalifa skyline cinematic`
- `Museum of the Future Dubai cinematic`
- `Dubai Frame skyline cinematic`
- `Dubai city lights aerial`
- `Dubai luxury residential tower exterior`

## Search Strategy

1. Read the brochure first. Extract: location, skyline references, amenities, design language, brand words, and exclusions.
2. Build searches from intent, not just literal nouns:
   - Location shots establish address.
   - Hospitality shots establish service and arrival.
   - Craft/material shots establish quality.
   - Amenity shots prove lifestyle.
   - Abstract backgrounds carry typography/logo moments.
3. Search multiple free sources. Prefer Pexels/Pixabay/Mixkit/Coverr first for no-watermark downloads. Use Videvo only after checking license and watermark status.
4. Download only clips that are license-safe for the use case and clean of visible watermarks.
5. For each downloaded clip, select only the best 1.5–5 seconds. Do not use full stock clips unless they are already perfectly paced.
6. Keep a source provenance file with URL, license summary, clip role, and chosen time range.

## Editing Rules

- Landscape outputs: target 1920×1080 16:9 unless user asks otherwise.
- Use elegant typography: high-contrast serif or refined geometric sans, generous letter spacing, minimal words.
- Cut on music beats. Use short black frames or black-screen flashes on key beats only; do not overuse.
- Use speed ramps, short crossfades, match cuts, and texture overlays to unify mixed stock.
- Color grade all clips to a coherent luxury palette: warm gold highlights, cream neutrals, charcoal blacks, controlled saturation, clean contrast.
- Reject brochure images as main footage unless explicitly requested. Logos from brochure/user image are okay for exact brand use.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Using full downloaded clips.** Stock clips are usually too long and generic. Trim to the strongest moments.
2. **Literal but irrelevant footage.** A luxury lobby or material detail can communicate the brand better than random apartment exteriors; but it must still map to project facts.
3. **Wrong Dubai vibe.** Palm/beach/desert/villa footage can weaken an urban SZR/Downtown/Jumeirah Garden City project.
4. **Watermarked previews.** Never use preview MP4s, thumbnails, or clips with visible stock-site branding.
5. **Logo distortion.** Do not ask AI to recreate brand text. Use the exact logo asset as overlay/outro and animate around it.
6. **Floor-plan contamination.** When the user says no floor plans, reject unit plan pages, plan graphics, and schematic layouts entirely.

## QA and Polish Before Delivery

A rendered MP4 is not automatically delivery-ready. For premium real-estate teasers, inspect contact sheets/sample frames before sending the file to the user.

- If a technically valid render contains a visually wrong segment, patch the weak segment rather than restarting the whole edit when the rest is acceptable.
- Reject quarry/mountain/raw outdoor stone when the intended role is interior material luxury or craftsmanship; replace with marble, fabric, wood veneer, designer samples, or artisan-hands close-ups.
- Reject non-Dubai/generic skyline shots for Dubai/SZR/Downtown/Jumeirah Garden City projects; replace with Burj Khalifa, Sheikh Zayed Road, Museum of the Future, Dubai Frame, Downtown/Business Bay, or credible Dubai high-rise context.
- Treat missing `shot_plan.md`, `source_provenance.md`, or `audit_summary.md` as a QA gap, even when final MP4s exist.
- See `references/qa-polish-patterns.md` for the detailed polish workflow.
